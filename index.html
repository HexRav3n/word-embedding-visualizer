Word Embedding Visualizer

Enter comma-separated words. Each word is converted into a 384-dimensional vector by a real sentence-embedding model running entirely in your browser (no API). The vectors are then projected to 3D using PCA and drawn as arrows from the origin. Click and drag the plot to rotate the view. Cosine similarity for every pair is shown on the right.

Idle. Click "Embed" to load the model and compute vectors. First load downloads ~25 MB.

3D Projection (PCA of 384D embeddings · drag to rotate)

Cosine Similarity (in full 384D space)

The arrows live in 384 dimensions; what you see is a shadow cast onto the three directions where the words vary the most. Pairs with high cosine similarity (closer to 1.0) point in similar directions in the real space, even if the 2D shadow looks misleading.
Model: Xenova/all-MiniLM-L6-v2 via transformers.js. If file:// blocks the import, serve the folder with python3 -m http.server and open http://localhost:8000.
